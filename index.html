What is SR&ED, and is my company eligible?

SR&ED — Scientific Research & Experimental Development — is a federal Canadian tax incentive administered by the Canada Revenue Agency. It rewards companies for performing R&D in Canada with refundable and non-refundable tax credits.

To qualify, work generally must involve technological uncertainty, a hypothesis, and systematic investigation aimed at advancing knowledge or capability. If you're building software, hardware, manufacturing processes, life-science products, or anything where the answer wasn't readily knowable up front — there's a strong chance some of that work qualifies.

How much can I actually recover?

It depends on your eligible expenditures (mostly wages, subcontractors, materials), your corporate structure, and your province. For Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPCs), the federal refundable rate is 35% on the first $3M of qualifying expenditures. Provincial credits stack on top.

Most SME claims we work on land between $50K and $200K+ in combined federal and provincial credits — but we'll give you a realistic range during your free assessment, not a guess.

What makes your fees lower than other SR&ED firms?

Two things. First, we run leaner — we don't carry the overhead of a national consulting practice, so we don't need to charge like one. Second, we believe a contingency fee should reflect the value of the work, not just what the market will tolerate.

Industry-standard rates sit around 15–25% of recovered credits. Our rate is set deliberately below that range, and we put the exact number in writing before you sign anything. No retainers, no hourly billing, no audit-defense upcharges.

How likely is a CRA audit, and what happens if I get one?

Review rates have climbed in recent years — meaningful enough that audit-defensible documentation isn't optional. CRA looks for clear technical narratives that map directly to the SR&ED criteria: uncertainty, hypothesis, systematic investigation, and advancement.

If your file is selected, we respond on your behalf — at no additional cost. The work we do up front is designed to answer CRA's questions before they ask, so most reviews resolve quickly.

Will this be a lot of work for my engineering or science team?

We aim for roughly 4–8 hours of total time from your technical leads, spread across the engagement. We come prepared with the right questions, take detailed interview notes, draft the narratives ourselves, and send them back for your team to review and correct — not write.

Do you work with my accountant?

Yes. We coordinate directly with your bookkeeper, controller, or external accountant on the financial side. The completed T661 is delivered ready to file with your T2 corporate return. Most accountants appreciate the partnership — SR&ED is specialized work that's outside the scope of a normal CPA engagement.

How long does the refund take to arrive?

For refundable claims with no review, CRA's published service standard is around 60 days from filing for first-time claimants and around 120 days otherwise. If your file is selected for review, expect a longer timeline — but a thoroughly documented claim moves faster through that process.

Can I claim SR&ED for work my company has already completed?

Yes — typically you can claim for work performed in the prior 18 months (within your last two filed tax years), depending on your fiscal year-end. If you're approaching a deadline, mention that on the consultation call so we can move quickly.
